How to use this keyboard

The special characters used in this orthography, and the key sequences to enter them with this keyboard, are

lower case upper case sequence
ḍ Ḍ d/D followed by asterisk
ṣ Ṣ s/S followed by asterisk
ñ Ñ n/N followed by tilde
ŋ Ŋ n/N followed by >
ĭ Ĭ i/I followed by caret (shift-6)
' (this symbol isn't cased) ' (straight apostrophe) followed by asterisk

Note that the word-forming apostrophe that is preferred by the Unicode standard is the modifier letter apostrophe U+02bc, not the straight apostrophe U+0027, used here to indicate a glottal stop.

The other non-English word-forming character used in this orthography is the colon, used to indicate vowel length. Since this can be entered with a normal US keyboard, no special keystroke is required for it.

The keystrokes are intended to resemble the keystrokes that are used in the SIL IPA Keyman keyboard.
